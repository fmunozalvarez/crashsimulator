CrashSimulator Best-Practice Drill Flow

1. Discover topology
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --discover
   Confirm role, open mode, CDB/PDB, RAC/GI, storage, Data Guard, APEX/ORDS, and ADB context.

2. Assess readiness
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --config-report --html
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --backup-report --html
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --maa-report --html
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --scenario-readiness-report --html

3. Pick the right scenario
   RUNNABLE: dry-run and execute with approval.
   PLAN-ONLY: collect runbook evidence, execution blocked by guardrail.
   NOT-RUNNABLE: fix topology, target context, service, or helper first.

4. Protect before risk
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --baseline-backup --execute
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --protect <id> --execute
   Capture RMAN tags, control-file autobackup, archive log posture, and restore path.

5. Dry-run and execute
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --scenario <id> --dry-run
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --scenario <id> --execute
   Require typed confirmation and approved non-production fault boundary.

6. Recover and stabilize
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --recover <id> --execute
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --health-check --pdb CRASHPDB
   ./CrashSimulatorV2.sh --backup-report --html

7. Preserve evidence
   Keep runbooks, manifests, audit records, SQL/RMAN logs, screenshots, reports, and HTML copies.
